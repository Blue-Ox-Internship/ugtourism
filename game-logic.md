Travel Like a Ugandan
Official Game Design Document (Version 1.0)
________________________________________
Overview
Travel Like a Ugandan is a fast-paced educational card game where players travel across Uganda by visiting destinations, experiencing cultures, tasting local foods, and meeting different communities.
The goal isn't to collect points.
The goal is to stay in the game by protecting your Destination Cards, while learning interesting facts about Uganda.
The game is designed for families, schools, tourists, youth groups, and anyone who wants to discover Uganda in a fun way.
________________________________________
Objective
Remain the last traveler with at least one Destination Card.
The first player to lose all their Destination Cards is eliminated.
The last remaining player wins.
________________________________________
Players

•	Recommended: 4–5 players 
________________________________________
Game Duration

Recommended Age
8+

Card Types
There are 5 card types.
________________________________________
1. Destination Cards
These represent places players have travelled to.
They are also the player's lives.
Examples
•	Murchison Falls 
•	Bwindi Forest 
•	Lake Mburo 
•	Source of the Nile 
•	Sipi Falls 
•	Rwenzori Mountains 
Every Destination card belongs to one region.
________________________________________
2. Community Cards
These represent Uganda's people.
Examples
•	Baganda 
•	Banyankole 
•	Basoga 
•	Acholi 
•	Iteso 
Each card teaches
•	greeting 
•	tribe 
•	language 
•	culture 
•	clothing 
•	traditions 
________________________________________
3. Experience Cards
Activities visitors can enjoy.
Examples
•	Gorilla Trekking 
•	Traditional Dance 
•	Boat Cruise 
•	Hiking 
•	Coffee Tour 
•	Market Visit 
Each card teaches a travel experience.
________________________________________
4. Taste Cards
Traditional foods and drinks.
Examples
•	Rolex 
•	Malewa 
•	Eshabwe 
•	Luwombo 
•	Atapa 
•	Millet Bread 
Each card contains a fun food fact.
________________________________________
5. Action Cards
Funny travel situations.
Unlike the other cards, these do not belong to any region.
Action cards are played immediately when drawn or chosen.
________________________________________
Regions
Every non-action card belongs to one of Uganda's five regions.
•	Central 
•	Western 
•	Northern 
•	Eastern 
•	West Nile (or if you prefer, keep "Northern" as one region and omit West Nile—be consistent across all cards) 
Use a large colored region icon on every card.
Example
🟢 Western
🔴 Central
🟡 Eastern
🔵 Northern
🟣 West Nile
This lets children identify regions without reading.
________________________________________
Setup
Separate the cards into two decks.
Destination Deck
Contains only Destination Cards.
Shuffle.
Place face down.
________________________________________
Main Deck
Contains
•	Community 
•	Experience 
•	Taste 
•	Action 
Shuffle together.
Place face down.
________________________________________
Each player receives
•	3 random Destination Cards 
•	2 Community Cards 
•	2 Experience Cards 
•	2 Taste Cards 
Players place their Destination Cards face up in front of them.
The remaining cards stay in the center.
________________________________________
Gameplay
The youngest player starts.
________________________________________
Step 1
The active player chooses one of their Destination Cards.
They place it in the center.
Before placing it, they must read the fact aloud.
Example
"Murchison Falls is where the Nile squeezes through a narrow gorge only seven metres wide."
Then they announce
"I'm travelling to Western Uganda!"
________________________________________
Step 2
Moving clockwise,
every other player must play one card from their hand that belongs to the same region.
It may be
•	Community 
•	Taste 
•	Experience 
Destination cards cannot be used.
________________________________________
Before placing the card,
the player must read the fact aloud.
________________________________________
If a player has no matching region
They draw one card from the Main Deck.
If the card matches the region,
they immediately play it.
If it doesn't,
they keep it in their hand.
The player who started the round immediately draws one new Destination Card from the Destination Deck.
This rewards successful travel planning.
________________________________________
Action Cards
If a player draws or already has an Action Card,
they may play it immediately instead of a regional card (or immediately upon drawing it, depending on the card wording).
When an Action Card is played:
•	Carry out its effect immediately. 
•	Nobody draws replacement cards for that interaction. This keeps Action Cards impactful without adding extra rules. 
After resolving the Action Card, play continues normally.
________________________________________
End of Round
After everyone has played,
the next player clockwise starts a new journey by playing one of their Destination Cards.
The process repeats.
________________________________________
When the Destination Deck is Empty
The rules change slightly.
Instead of drawing a new Destination Card,
the active player steals one random Destination Card from any player who failed to match the region.
This keeps the tension high throughout the game.
________________________________________
Elimination
A player who loses their last Destination Card
has completed their journey and leaves the game.
________________________________________
Winning
The last player with at least one Destination Card
wins.
________________________________________
Golden Rule
Before playing any non-action card, the player must read the information on it aloud.
This is the heart of the game.
Everyone learns together.

Action Cards
These should be the funniest cards in the game.
Examples
________________________________________
Wrong Greeting
You greeted the village elder incorrectly.
Pass one Destination Card to the player on your right.
________________________________________
Missed Dance Step
You missed a step during the Acholi dance.
Lose one Destination Card.
________________________________________
Friendly Tour Guide
A local guide helped you.
Take one random card from the Main Deck.
________________________________________
Heavy Rain
Your safari is delayed.
Miss your next turn.
________________________________________
Boda Boda Shortcut
You reached your destination quickly.
Choose the next region by selecting one of your Destination Cards to start immediately after this round.
________________________________________
Festival Invitation
You made new friends.
Swap one card in your hand with another player.
________________________________________
Design Philosophy
The game follows four principles:
1. Learn Naturally
No quizzes.
No tests.
Just read the card.
________________________________________
2. Laugh Together
Action Cards create memorable stories.
________________________________________
3. Simple Rules
Anyone should learn the game in under five minutes.
________________________________________
4. Every Card Teaches Something
There are no "empty" cards.
Every card adds knowledge.
