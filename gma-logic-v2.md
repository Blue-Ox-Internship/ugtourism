Travel Like a Ugandan
Discover Uganda, One Card at a Time.

Game Design Document – Version 3.0

1. Game Overview

Travel Like a Ugandan is a fast-paced educational card game where players journey across Uganda by visiting destinations, experiencing cultures, meeting communities, tasting traditional foods, and overcoming travel adventures.

The game combines strategy, luck and learning in a simple, family-friendly format.

Unlike many educational games, learning happens naturally because every card tells a story or teaches a fact before it is played.

2. Objective

Become the last traveller remaining by managing your Destination Cards wisely and always being prepared for the next journey.

The winner is not necessarily the player with the most Destination Cards, but the player who can continue travelling when everyone else can no longer do so.

9. Setup

Separate the game into two decks.

Destination Deck

Contains only Destination Cards.

Shuffle and place face down.

Main Deck

Contains

Community
Experience
Taste
Journey
Action

Shuffle and place face down.

Each player receives

3 Destination Cards
2 Community Cards
2 Experience Cards
2 Taste Cards

Players display their three Destination Cards face-up in front of them.

The remaining cards stay in their respective decks.

Choose any player to begin.

Play proceeds clockwise.

10. The Journey

The game is played in rounds called Journeys.

Step 1

The active player chooses one of their displayed Destination Cards and places it in the centre of the table.

This card starts the Journey.

Before placing it, the player must read the educational fact aloud.

Example

"Sipi Falls consists of three spectacular waterfalls on Mount Elgon."

The player announces:

"Let's travel to Eastern Uganda!"

The Destination Card remains in the centre as part of the Journey Pile.

It does not return to the player.

Step 2

Beginning with the next player clockwise,

every player must play

a Community card,
an Experience card,
a Taste card,

or

a Journey Card

that matches the Destination's region.

Before playing,

the player must read the fact aloud.

Their card is placed on top of the Journey Pile.

11. If a Player Cannot Match the Region

If a player has no matching card,

they draw one card from the Main Deck.

If that card matches the region (or is a Journey Card),

they immediately play it.

If it still cannot be played,

they keep it in their hand.

For every player who fails to complete the journey,

the player who started that Journey earns one Destination Card from the Destination Deck.

This reward happens immediately.

12. Action Cards

Action Cards may be played immediately when they are drawn or when a player chooses to use one from their hand, according to the card's instructions.

After resolving the Action Card:

follow the effect immediately,
no replacement cards are drawn because of the Action Card,
play then continues normally.

Action Cards are designed to create fun, unexpected travel moments without interrupting the flow of the game.

13. Journey Pile

Each completed Journey remains in the centre of the table.

The next player starts a new Journey by placing one of their Destination Cards on top of the existing pile.

As the game progresses, the centre pile becomes a record of everyone's travels across Uganda.

14. When the Destination Deck is Empty

Once the Destination Deck has been exhausted,

players can no longer earn new Destination Cards from the deck.

Instead,

whenever a player fails to complete a Journey,

the player who started that Journey randomly takes one Destination Card from that player's remaining displayed Destination Cards.

15. Elimination

A player is eliminated immediately when they have no Destination Cards remaining.

They leave the game.

16. Winning

The last player with at least one Destination Card is declared the winner.

Victory comes from balancing your Destination Cards with a hand that allows you to keep travelling across Uganda.

17. Golden Rule

Before playing any non-Action card,

the player must read the educational fact aloud.

Learning is part of every Journey.

18. Strategy

Winning requires players to balance two resources:

Destination Cards

These keep you in the game.

However,

having many Destination Cards alone does not guarantee victory.

Travel Cards

(Community, Experience, Taste and Journey Cards)

These allow you to complete Journeys started by other players.

Running out of these cards can quickly cause you to lose Destination Cards, even if you have many remaining.

Success comes from balancing both resources.

19. Design Philosophy

The game is built around five principles:

Simple to Learn – Rules can be explained in under five minutes.
Educational Through Play – Every card teaches something about Uganda.
Strategic but Accessible – Players must manage both Destination Cards and their travel cards.
Conversation Starter – Reading cards aloud encourages discussion and storytelling.
Celebrate Uganda – Every Journey showcases Uganda's places, people, traditions and food.

After writing nearly 60 cards, I noticed something that could make the game feel even more premium.

Instead of putting "Region: Western" on every card, what if each region had its own visual identity?

For example:

🟢 Central → Royal Bark Cloth pattern
🔵 Western → Ankole long-horn-inspired pattern
🟡 Northern → Woven basket or spear motif
🔴 Eastern → Mount Elgon or Imbalu-inspired pattern

Players would recognize the region instantly from the border before even reading the text. It reinforces the identity of each region while keeping the layout clean.

I think that's the kind of detail that would make someone pick up Travel Like a Ugandan on a shelf and immediately feel that it celebrates Uganda, rather than just using Uganda as a theme.
