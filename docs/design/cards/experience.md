# Experience Cards

## Role in the Game
Experience cards represent activities and travel experiences visitors can enjoy across Uganda. Played during a Journey to match the active Destination's region. The player reads the card's fact aloud before playing — teaching everyone something about that experience or activity.

## Count
**24 cards** in the deck. Each player starts with 2.

---

## Front Design

### Layout
- **Top left** — Region badge: colored circle with bold letter (C / W / N / E)
- **Top right** — Small label: "Experience" in uppercase tracking
- **Center** — Full bleed painterly illustration of people actively doing the experience
- **Bottom** — Dark gradient overlay with experience name (bold serif) and region below

### Illustration Direction
Action, movement, joy. Show people in the middle of the experience — not a static landscape. A rafter mid-rapid, a hiker reaching a peak, a dancer mid-step, a boat on the Nile at sunset. Energy and motion. Warm tones, natural light.

| Card | Fact | Region |
|---|---|---|
| Cultural Dance Night | The Ndere Centre in Kampala hosts weekly performances showcasing dances from all of Uganda's regions in one evening. | Central |
| Market Visit | Owino Market in Kampala is one of East Africa's largest open-air markets, with over 60,000 vendors operating daily. | Central |
| Craft Shopping | Uganda's Craft Village in Kampala is where local artisans sell bark cloth, beadwork, and hand-carved wooden crafts. | Central |
| Kasubi Tombs Tour | The Kasubi Tombs are the burial grounds of four Buganda kings — a living royal shrine, not just a museum. | Central |

| Kampala Food Walk | A guided walk through Kampala's street food scene — Rolex stands, nyama choma grills, and fresh tropical fruits. | Central |
