# Journey Cards (Universal Cards)

## Role in the Game
Journey cards are wildcards. Also called **Universal Cards**, they can be played on **any region** — no region restriction. A player who has no Community, Experience, or Taste card matching the active region can play a Journey card instead. They cannot replace Destination cards.

The "U" on the front stands for **Universal** — these cards belong to no single region because every traveller carries them regardless of where they go.

## Count
**10 cards** in the deck. None dealt at the start — drawn from the Main Deck during play.

## Front Design

### Layout
- **Top left** — Region badge: silver/white circle with bold letter **"U"** (Universal — no region color, neutral)
- **Top right** — Small label: "Journey" in uppercase tracking
- **Center** — Full bleed painterly illustration of the travel item or tool
- **Bottom** — Dark gradient overlay with card name (bold serif) and "Universal Card" below in smaller italic

### Illustration Direction
Objects and tools, not places or people. Warm, detailed, almost still-life in quality. The camera, the backpack, the map — illustrated as if they are beloved, well-used travel companions. Vintage travel poster meets board game illustration. Slightly warmer and more intimate than the Destination card illustrations.

### Special Visual Detail
A subtle **golden glow or shimmer** around the "U" badge and card border distinguishes Journey cards as special. When a player plays one, it should feel like pulling out a useful tool — satisfying, smart, a little bit lucky.
