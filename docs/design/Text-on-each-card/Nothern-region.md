🗺️ DESTINATION CARDS (Northern)
1. Murchison Falls National Park
🌊 Nature's Mighty Roar

The River Nile squeezes through a narrow 7-metre gorge before crashing down about 43 metres, creating one of the world's most powerful waterfalls.

2. Kidepo Valley National Park
🦓 Uganda's Wild Frontier

Tucked away in Uganda's northeast, Kidepo is famous for its vast open plains, rugged mountains, and incredible wildlife, earning it a reputation as one of Africa's finest safari parks.

3. Aruu Falls
💦 Hidden Cascades

Aruu Falls is a series of beautiful waterfalls where water tumbles over smooth rocks, creating a peaceful escape in northern Uganda.

4. Ziwa Rhino Sanctuary
🦏 Where Rhinos Roam Again

Ziwa Rhino Sanctuary is the only place in Uganda where you can see wild rhinos on foot as part of the country's efforts to restore the Big Five.

(Although located in Nakasongola, officially in the Central Region, Ziwa lies on the main route to Murchison Falls. If you prefer strict regional accuracy, we can replace this with another Northern destination such as Ajai Wildlife Reserve.)

5. Ajai Wildlife Reserve
🦒 A Quiet Wilderness

Named after a local chief, Ajai Wildlife Reserve protects wetlands and savannah that are home to antelopes, birds, and other wildlife.

6. Karuma Falls
🌊 The Nile's Rushing Waters

Karuma Falls is a dramatic stretch of rapids where the Nile races over rocky outcrops before continuing its journey north.

7. Fort Patiko (Baker's Fort)
🏰 Echoes of History

Built in the 19th century, Fort Patiko tells stories of explorers, trade, and Uganda's rich past.

8. Pian Upe Wildlife Reserve
🦌 Uganda's Hidden Savannah

One of Uganda's largest protected areas, Pian Upe is home to cheetahs, ostriches, antelopes, and breathtaking open landscapes.

(Pian Upe is officially in the Eastern Region. For regional consistency, we should instead use Mount Moroto or another northern destination if we want strict adherence to the four-region model.)

👥 COMMUNITY CARDS (Northern)
1. Acholi
🥁 Proud Storytellers

The Acholi people are known for their rich traditions of storytelling, music, dance, and community celebrations.

Pronunciation

The Langi are known for their strong farming traditions and close-knit communities in northern Uganda.

4. Traditional Drumming
🥁 Feel the Rhythm

Drums are central to many northern celebrations, bringing communities together through music and dance.

5. Bwola Dance
💃 Dance of Unity

Bwola is a famous Acholi royal dance traditionally performed during important ceremonies and celebrations.

6. Weaving
🧺 Crafted by Hand

Handwoven baskets and mats showcase generations of skill and creativity passed from one generation to the next.

7. Hospitality
🤝 Visitors Are Welcome

🎒 EXPERIENCE CARDS (Northern)
1. Game Drive
🦁 Safari Adventure

Watch giraffes, elephants, buffaloes, and lions roam freely across Uganda's northern savannah.

2. Cultural Dance Performance
🎶 Join the Celebration

Experience energetic dances accompanied by drums, songs, and traditional instruments.

3. Bird Watching
🦅 Eyes on the Sky

Northern Uganda is home to hundreds of bird species, making it a paradise for bird lovers.

4. Boat Safari
🚤 Cruise the Nile

Travel along the River Nile and spot crocodiles, hippos, elephants, and colourful birds from the water.

5. Village Visit
🛖 Meet the Community

Spend time learning local traditions, farming practices, and everyday life from the people who call northern Uganda home.

6. Nature Walk
🌿 Slow Down and Explore

A guided walk reveals unique plants, animal tracks, and hidden wonders often missed on a vehicle safari.

7. Camp Under the Stars
⭐ Sleep in the Wild

As night falls, listen to the sounds of nature beneath some of Uganda's clearest skies.

🍽️ TASTE CARDS (Northern)
1. Malakwang
🍃 A Northern Favourite

Malakwang is a delicious sour vegetable dish often cooked with groundnut paste and enjoyed with millet bread.

2. Millet Bread
🌾 Made to Share

Millet bread has long been a staple meal in northern Uganda and is often shared during family gatherings.

3. Boo
🥬 Simple and Nutritious

Boo is a traditional leafy vegetable commonly prepared with groundnuts or sesame.

4. Simsim Paste
🥜 Rich in Flavour

Made from sesame seeds, simsim paste adds a creamy, nutty taste to many northern dishes.

5. Atapa
🍚 Comfort on a Plate

Atapa is made by mixing cassava flour with millet flour to create a smooth, filling meal enjoyed across northern Uganda.

6. Roasted Groundnuts
🥜 Snack Time

Freshly roasted groundnuts are a popular snack enjoyed while travelling or relaxing with friends.

7. Shea Butter
🌰 Nature's Gift

In parts of northern Uganda, shea nuts are traditionally processed into butter used for cooking, skincare, and cultural practices.
