🗺️ DESTINATION CARDS (Eastern)
1. Source of the Nile
🌍 Where the Nile Begins

Jinja is home to the Source of the Nile, where the world's longest river begins its incredible journey through Africa to the Mediterranean Sea.

2. Sipi Falls
💦 Three Waterfalls, One Adventure

Sipi Falls is made up of three beautiful waterfalls flowing down the slopes of Mount Elgon, making it one of Uganda's most scenic destinations.

3. Mount Elgon National Park
🏔️ An Ancient Giant

Mount Elgon is one of the world's oldest extinct volcanoes and is home to waterfalls, caves, forests, and breathtaking hiking trails.

4. Nyero Rock Paintings
🎨 Messages from the Past

The Nyero Rock Paintings are ancient artworks believed to be thousands of years old, offering a glimpse into Uganda's early history.

5. Busitema Forest
🌳 Nature's Classroom

Busitema Forest is home to many bird species and native trees, making it a peaceful place to explore nature.

6. Wanale Ridge
🌄 Above the Clouds

Climb Wanale Ridge for sweeping views of Mbale City and the surrounding plains beneath Mount Elgon.

7. Sisiyi Falls
🌿 A Hidden Treasure

Nestled near Mount Elgon, Sisiyi Falls rewards visitors with cool mountain air and beautiful natural scenery.

8. Tororo Rock
🪨 The Rock That Watches Over the Town

Towering above Tororo Town, this massive rock is one of Eastern Uganda's most recognizable landmarks and offers spectacular views from the top.

👥 COMMUNITY CARDS (Eastern)
1. Basoga
🌊 People of the Nile

The Basoga are one of Uganda's largest communities and are well known for their rich culture, music, and connection to the River Nile.

🗣️ Learn a Greeting

Word
Wasuze otya?

Pronunciation
(wah-SOO-zeh oh-TYAH)

Meaning
Good morning (How did you spend the night?)

When to Use
Use this when greeting someone in the morning.

2. Bagisu
⛰️ Guardians of Mount Elgon

The Bagisu, also called the Bamasaba, are famous for living on the fertile slopes of Mount Elgon.

🗣️ Learn a Greeting

Word
Mulembe

Pronunciation
(moo-LEM-bay)

Meaning
Peace / Hello.

When to Use
A respectful greeting commonly heard around eastern Uganda.

3. Iteso
🌾 People of the Plains

The Iteso are known for farming, storytelling, and preserving traditions through music and dance.

🗣️ Learn a Greeting

Word
Ejoka?

Pronunciation
(eh-JOH-kah)

Meaning
How are you?

When to Use
Use this to greet someone in Ateso.

4. Imbalu
🎉 A Celebration of Courage

Imbalu is the traditional initiation ceremony of the Bagisu, celebrated with music, dancing, and community gatherings.

5. Kadodi Dance
🥁 Dance to the Drumbeat

Kadodi is an energetic traditional dance performed during Imbalu celebrations, with powerful drum rhythms filling the air.

6. Basket Weaving
🧺 Crafted with Care

Beautiful woven baskets are made by skilled artisans using natural fibres passed down through generations.

7. Hospitality
🤝 Every Guest Matters

Visitors are warmly welcomed and often invited to share stories, food, and laughter.

🍽️ TASTE CARDS (Eastern)
1. Malewa
🌿 A Taste of Mount Elgon

Malewa is made from smoked bamboo shoots and is one of Eastern Uganda's most famous traditional dishes.

2. Smoked Fish
🐟 Fresh from the Water

Fish from Lake Kyoga and the Nile are often smoked to preserve their rich flavour.

3. Sweet Potatoes
🍠 Naturally Sweet

Eastern Uganda's fertile soils produce delicious sweet potatoes enjoyed in homes across the region.

4. Groundnut Sauce
🥜 A Family Favourite

Groundnut sauce is rich, creamy, and pairs perfectly with many traditional meals.

5. Cassava
🌱 Simple and Filling

Boiled, fried, or dried into flour, cassava is an important staple food across Eastern Uganda.

6. Millet Porridge
🥣 Start the Day Right

A warm bowl of millet porridge is a popular breakfast enjoyed by many families.

7. Fresh Fruit
🥭 Nature's Sweetness

Eastern Uganda's warm climate produces delicious mangoes, pineapples, passion fruits, and oranges.
