COMMUNITY CARDS (Central)
1. Baganda

The Baganda are Uganda's largest ethnic community and are known for their rich traditions, kingdoms, and warm hospitality.

2. Greeting

In Luganda, "Oli otya?" means "How are you?" A warm greeting is often the first step to making a good impression.

3. Bark Cloth

Made from the mutuba tree, bark cloth has been used in Buganda for centuries for clothing, ceremonies, and cultural traditions.

4. The Kabaka

The King of Buganda is called the Kabaka, a title that has been passed down for generations.

5. Clans

Every Muganda belongs to a clan. Clans help preserve family history, identity, and traditions.

6. Bakisimba Dance

Bakisimba is one of Buganda's most famous traditional dances, often performed during celebrations and royal ceremonies.

7. Hospitality

In many Buganda homes, visitors are welcomed warmly before any business or conversation begins.

EXPERIENCE CARDS (Central)
1. City Tour

Kampala is a city where modern life and tradition meet. Every neighbourhood has a story waiting to be discovered.

2. Cultural Dance

Join a traditional dance performance and experience Uganda through music, rhythm, and movement.

3. Craft Market

Colourful baskets, wood carvings, jewellery, and paintings make excellent souvenirs to remember your journey.

4. Boat Ride on Lake Victoria

Africa's largest lake offers peaceful cruises, fishing adventures, and beautiful sunsets.

5. Museum Visit

Discover ancient tools, musical instruments, and cultural treasures from across Uganda.

6. Forest Walk

Walking beneath Mabira Forest's tall trees is a chance to hear birdsong and spot playful monkeys.

7. Coffee Stop

Uganda is one of Africa's leading coffee producers. Enjoying a fresh cup is part of the journey.

TASTE CARDS (Central)
1. Rolex

Freshly cooked eggs wrapped in warm chapati—Uganda's famous Rolex is loved from roadside stalls to city streets.

2. Luwombo

Slow-cooked in banana leaves, Luwombo is a treasured Buganda dish full of rich flavour.

3. Katogo

A hearty breakfast made by cooking matooke or cassava together with meat, beans, or offal.

4. Roasted Gonja

Sweet roasted plantains are a favourite roadside snack enjoyed across Central Uganda.

5. Fresh Sugarcane Juice

Freshly pressed sugarcane juice is naturally sweet and refreshing on a warm afternoon.

6. Groundnut Sauce

Creamy groundnut sauce is a delicious companion to matooke, rice, cassava, or sweet potatoes.

7. Fresh Tilapia

Fish freshly caught from Lake Victoria is often grilled whole and shared with family and friends.
